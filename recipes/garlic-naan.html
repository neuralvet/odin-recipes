Garlic Naan

Description

This is a fairly simple way to make garlic naan. I sometimes cook it outside on my Blackstone. Other times, I will cook it in a cast iron skillet. Keep in mind that if you cook indoors, it may get a bit smokey.

Ingredients

  • 2tsp Active Dry yeast
  • 1/2 TBSP sugar
  • 1 cup warm tap water
  • 1/2 cup milk (lukewarm)
  • 1tsp salt
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 2 TBSP olive oil
  • 360 grams All Purpose floud (about 3 cups)

Steps

  1. Mix yeast, water, and sugard together in a bowl. Let the yeast bloom
  2. Add milk, salt, garlic paste, and oil to another bowl. I whiz this up in the microwave for a few seconds to warm the milk up as it should be “lukewarm”.
  3. Add contents of both bowls toger and hand mix. If it is too wet, add some flour. Knead 1-2 minutes in the bowl. The dough will be a bit wet; it should not be very dry.
  4. Cover and let rise about 1.5 hours.
  5. Punch down and transfer to flour dusted surface.
  6. Cut into 8 pieces. Roll out and cook on on stovetop.
  7. Brush with garlic, butter, olive oil mixure while cooking.
